First Aid Basics

First aid is the immediate assistance given to someone who is injured or suddenly becomes ill. It can save lives, prevent further harm, and promote recovery. Here are some key points to remember:

  • Stay Calm: Assess the situation and ensure your safety first.
  • Call for Help: Dial emergency services if needed.
  • Provide Care: Follow the appropriate first aid steps.

First Aid Steps & Remedies

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

  1. Check for responsiveness and breathing.
  2. Call emergency services.
  3. Perform chest compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute.
  4. Give rescue breaths if trained.

Bleeding Control

  1. Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth.
  2. Elevate the injured area if possible.
  3. Use a bandage to secure the cloth.
  4. Seek medical help if bleeding doesn't stop.

Burn Treatment

  1. Cool the burn under running water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Cover the burn with a sterile, non-stick dressing.
  3. Avoid applying ice or creams.
  4. Seek medical attention for severe burns.
